excerpt: 'Self-folding offers a manufacturing-to-deployment route for architected metamaterials: you print flat, then trigger a controlled transformation into complex 3D load-bearing structures. Most self-folding metamaterials perform only one transformation, which locks them into a single geometry and a narrow mechanical response. We designed a cage-shaped self-folding metamaterial that can be programmed to realize multiple stable 3D configurations from the same initially flat sheet. By heating to distinct thresholds, the structure sequentially folds into different modes and retains each configuration after cooling (stateful morphing rather than transient actuation). We engineered two classes of self-folding elements with different thermal thresholds (leveraging material-dependent transition behavior), enabling staged folding within one unit cell. We built a mechanics model that links geometry and folding mode to force–displacement response, allowing mechanical performance to be designed rather than empirically tuned. Experiments and FEM show mode-dependent force–displacement curves with multi-stage load-bearing; importantly, multi-level cages behave like parallel nonlinear springs, enabling decoupled design by independently tuning each level and superimposing responses.'

This paper presents our research on cage-shaped self-folding mechanical metamaterials, a significant advancement in the field of programmable materials.

**Research Background:**
Self-folding mechanical metamaterials represent a cutting-edge approach to creating materials with programmable mechanical properties. Traditional metamaterials are often limited to single transformation modes, restricting their versatility in practical applications.

**Key Contributions:**
- **Multi-stage Transformation:** Our cage-shaped design enables multiple deformation modes triggered by different temperature thresholds
- **Programmable Properties:** Theoretical models allow for precise control over mechanical performance
- **Stable Configurations:** The material maintains its transformed state upon cooling, enabling practical applications
- **Tunable Performance:** Mechanical properties can be adjusted for different use cases

**Technical Innovation:**
The metamaterial consists of temperature-responsive self-folding elements that achieve different folding thresholds. This design allows for:
- Multi-stage load-bearing capabilities
- Decoupling of mechanical performance parameters
- Multifunctional applications across various fields

**Applications:**
This research opens new possibilities for:
- Adaptive structural materials
- Programmable mechanical systems
- Multi-functional engineering applications
- Temperature-responsive devices

**Impact:**
Published in the prestigious International Journal of Solids and Structures, this work contributes to the growing field of programmable materials and demonstrates the potential for creating truly adaptive mechanical systems.

**Research Team:**
This work was conducted in collaboration with researchers from Tsinghua University, demonstrating the power of interdisciplinary collaboration in advancing materials science.
